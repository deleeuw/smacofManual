Here are some analyses of the Rothkopf Morse data, using a variety of
loss functions. All analyses are metric and two-dimensional. We used the
assymetric confusion matrix and defined a dissimilarity by the
Shepard-Luce like formula \[
\delta_{ij}=-\log\frac{p_{ij}p_{ji}}{p_{ii}p_{jj}}.
\]

The fivenum for these data is

\begin{verbatim}
[1] 0.000000 2.438489 3.944610 5.329541 8.350312
\end{verbatim}

Thus data are fairly symmetric, a bit skewed to the left, with a median
around four and values between zero and ten,

There are four analyses.

\begin{enumerate}
\def\labelenumi{\arabic{enumi}.}
\tightlist
\item
  Huber with cut-off 25, well outside the range, de facto least squares
  loss.
\item
  Huber with cut-off .01, de facto least absolute value loss.
\item
  Huber with cut-off 4, around the median.
\item
  Tukey with cut-off 4, around the median.
\end{enumerate}

In the last three analyses, especially in the fourth, the large
residuals are down-weighted. An inifinite number of other analyses are
possible. I am taking requests.
